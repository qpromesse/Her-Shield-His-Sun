Chapter 23: "Pressure"

Chapter Text The argument about the calibration components lasts forty-five minutes and ends with nobody winning. This is, in Toma's experience, the correct outcome of any argument with Clara, who does not argue to win and does not argue to lose — she argues to be accurate, which is a completely different objective and one that is deeply annoying to argue against because you cannot defeat it, you can only be more accurate than her, which Toma has not yet managed to do on any topic related to workshop equipment. "I'm not saying I want to use them for the route maps," Toma says, for the third time, with the emphatic patience of someone who has been misunderstood twice and is trying a third approach. "I'm saying the signal frequency range of the calibration component is similar to the range of the tunnel sensor Luka uses, and if the tunnel sensor can map distance to a signal source then why can't the calibration component do the same thing for our salvage routes—" "Because the calibration component is calibrated," Clara says. "That's the whole point of it. It's set to a specific frequency. If you use it to map a different frequency it stops being calibrated and then it can't do what it's supposed to do." "Can't you just recalibrate it after?" "Yes, but Svarog would—" "Would what?" Clara looks at the component in her hand. She looks at Toma. She makes the face — the face of someone who knows the answer and wishes the answer were different. "He would be very calm about it," she says. "He would explain exactly why it is not supposed to be used that way, and he would do it at the length and thoroughness that Svarog explains things." Toma stares at her. "How long is that?" "Long," Clara says, with the flat expressiveness of someone speaking from experience.
